   From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
   Need to sacrifice consistency or availability in the presence of
   network partitions

   In
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theoretical_computer_science]theoretical
   computer science, the CAP theorem, also named Brewer's theorem after
   computer scientist
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Brewer_(scientist)]Eric Brewer,
   states that any
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_data_store]distributed data
   store can provide only [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trilemma]two of
   the following three
   guarantees:^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Gilber
   t_Lynch-1][1]^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-2][2
   ]^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-3][3]

   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consistency_model]Consistency
          Every read receives the most recent write or an error.

   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Availability]Availability
          Every request receives a (non-error) response, without the
          guarantee that it contains the most recent write.

   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_partitioning]Partition tolerance
          The system continues to operate despite an arbitrary number of
          messages being dropped (or delayed) by the network between
          nodes.

   When a [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_partition]network
   partition failure happens, it must be decided whether to do one of the
   following:
     * cancel the operation and thus decrease the availability but ensure
       consistency
     * proceed with the operation and thus provide availability but risk
       inconsistency.

   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:CAP_Theorem_Venn_Diagram.png][220px
   -CAP_Theorem_Venn_Diagram.png]
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:CAP_Theorem_Venn_Diagram.png]
   CAP Theorem Venn Diagram

   Thus, if there is a network partition, one has to choose between
   consistency or availability. Note that consistency as defined in the
   CAP theorem is quite different from the consistency guaranteed in
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACID]ACID
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database_transaction]database
   transactions.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-4][4
   ]

Explanation[[https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=CAP_theorem&action=ed
it&section=1]edit]

   No distributed system is safe from network failures, thus network
   partitioning generally has to be
   tolerated.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-5][5]^[
   https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-6][6] In the
   presence of a partition, one is then left with two options: consistency
   or [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Availability]availability. When
   choosing consistency over availability, the system will return an error
   or a time out if particular information cannot be guaranteed to be up
   to date due to network partitioning. When choosing availability over
   consistency, the system will always process the query and try to return
   the most recent available version of the information, even if it cannot
   guarantee it is up to date due to network partitioning.

   In the absence of a partition, both availability and consistency can be
   satisfied.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-paclec-
   7][7]

   Database systems designed with traditional
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACID]ACID guarantees in mind such as
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relational_database_management_system]RD
   BMS choose
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consistency_(database_systems)]consisten
   cy over availability, whereas systems designed around the
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eventual_consistency]BASE philosophy,
   common in the [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NoSQL]NoSQL movement for
   example, choose availability over
   consistency.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Brewe
   r2012-8][8]

History[[https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=CAP_theorem&action=edit&s
ection=2]edit]

   According to
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley]Unive
   rsity of California, Berkeley computer scientist
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eric_Brewer_(scientist)]Eric Brewer, the
   theorem first appeared in autumn
   1998.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Brewer2012-8
   ][8] It was published as the CAP principle in
   1999^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Brewer1999-9]
   [9] and presented as a
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjecture]conjecture by Brewer at the
   2000
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symposium_on_Principles_of_Distributed_C
   omputing]Symposium on Principles of Distributed Computing
   (PODC).^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Brewer2000
   -10][10] In 2002,
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Seth_Gilbert&action=edit&re
   dlink=1]Seth Gilbert and
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nancy_Lynch]Nancy Lynch of
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIT]MIT published a formal proof of
   Brewer's conjecture, rendering it a
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theorem]theorem.^[https://en.wikipedia.o
   rg/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Gilbert_Lynch-1][1]

   In 2012, Brewer clarified some of his positions, including why the
   often-used "two out of three" concept can be somewhat misleading
   because system designers only need to sacrifice consistency or
   availability in the presence of partitions; partition management and
   recovery techniques exist. Brewer also noted the different definition
   of consistency used in the CAP theorem relative to the definition used
   in
   [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACID]ACID.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wik
   i/CAP_theorem#cite_note-Brewer2012-8][8]^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki
   /CAP_theorem#cite_note-:0-11][11]

   A similar theorem stating the trade-off between consistency and
   availability in distributed systems was published by Birman and
   Friedman in
   1996.^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-12][12]
   Birman and Friedman's result restricted this lower bound to
   non-commuting operations.

   The [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PACELC_theorem]PACELC theorem,
   introduced in
   2010,^[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem#cite_note-paclec-7][7]
   builds on CAP by stating that even in the absence of partitioning,
   there is another trade-off between latency and consistency. PACELC
   means, if partition (P) happens, the trade-off is between availability
   (A) and consistency (C); Else (E), the trade-off is between latency (L)
   and consistency (C).
